Build a strong foundation in Lean thinking as you develop personal goals for effective leadership

Course Description

Course Number BE901: Fundamentals of Lean Leadership: Foundations in effective Lean leadership

Understanding the core principles of Lean, Lean Leadership, and the development of your own goals to become a more effective leader. Understand an integrative model of leadership that transforms you from an (old thinking) manager to an empowering leader. Create a plan for your own leadership development.

Course Topics

  • Why Lean & the role of Leadership
    • Purpose and objectives of Lean
    • The History of Lean and its impact on Lean Thinking
    • Building a Lean Enterprise: Principles, Planning Culture, and Tools
    • The role of leadership in a Lean Enterprise
    • Creating a Learning Organization
    • The Leadership Spiral & the management vs. leadership debate
  • Elements of Lean Leadership
    • Lean Thinking
    • Elements of Lean Leadership
      • Establishing Vision & Alignment
      • Developing a Continuous Improvement Culture
      • Creating a Favorable Environment
  • Setting Goals for yourself: Open Window Planning Method
    • How to use the Open Window Planning Chart
    • The Lean Leadership Inventory and how you score
    • Creation of your own Goals
  • Getting Started: Some Fundamental Skills
    • Skill One: Identify the Right Focus
    • Skill Two: Learn by Going to the Gemba
    • Skill Three: Practice respect by Involving People
    • Skill Four: See and Build Leaders
    • Skill Five: Develop Teamwork to bring everyone onboard
  • Taking It Back Home
    • Creating an Action Plan to get started

Course Objectives

Upon completion of this one-day course, participants will have a strong foundation in Lean leadership principles and Lean methodologies

What career paths benefit the most from Fundamentals of Lean Leadership Workshop?

  • Business professionals and practitioners interested in learning how Lean can improve productivity, lead time and quality and make their day to day activities easier and more fulfilling
  • People working with manufacturing or non-manufacturing processes
  • Executive management or for profit and non-profit organizations
  • Administrative personnel who own or participate in process development and execution
