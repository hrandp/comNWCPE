Course Description

Course Number BE809 Learning to Improve — the Kaizen Way: Creating a Learning Culture through Continuous Improvement

Authentic Lean requires continuous improvement — every day, by every person. To achieve continuous improvement an organization needs a reliable improvement method. The objective of this training is to provide you with the necessary skills and knowledge to implement continuous improvement in the workplace. Students will learn how to align improvement activities, create an improvement focus, and perform every day problem-solving with PDSA and small tests of change. The course is energy-packed and hands-on; participants will practice the concepts and tools in a simulation.

Course Topics

  • Overview of the Reliable Method for Improvement (SBAR/PDSA) & how it works.
  • PDSA (The Plan Do Study Act Method).
  • How to Align with Key Performance Indicators and create a Focus for Improvement.
  • Creating a Plan for testing and implementing improvement solutions.
  • How to test and study a solution.
  • Acting on your test: Adopt, Adapt, or Abandon.
  • Creating controls and new standards.

Course Objectives

Upon completion of this one-day course, participants will have learned or reinforced the concepts of Lean continuous improvement.

What career paths benefit the most from Learning to Improve — the Kaizen Way Workshop?

  • Business professionals and practitioners interested in learning how Lean can improve productivity, lead time and quality and make their day to day activities easier and more fulfilling
  • People working with manufacturing or non-manufacturing processes
  • Executive management or for profit and non-profit organizations
  • Administrative personnel who own or participate in process development and execution

Course Facilitator:

Tom Fabrizio is the author of several books on Lean and a regular instructor at Portland State University’s Professional Development Center. He has helped both large and small companies of all industries refine their processes through Continuous Improvement and Lean methodologies. Originally trained by the developers of Lean practices, including Shigeo Shingo from Toyota, Deming Prize-winner Ryuji Fukuda, and the founders of the Japan Institute of Plant Maintenance, he holds an engineering degree from Tufts University, a Masters Degree in Education from Northeastern University, and Authentic Leadership certification from Naropa University.
